YouTube OAuth Setup Guide

Connect your YouTube account to live stream directly from VistterStream — no stream keys needed.

What is YouTube OAuth?
OAuth lets VistterStream create and manage YouTube Live broadcasts on your behalf. Instead of manually copying stream keys from YouTube Studio, VistterStream handles everything automatically.

Before You Start

Make sure you have:

  • A YouTube account that is enabled for live streaming (YouTube requires you to verify your channel — this can take up to 24 hours the first time)
  • Access to VistterStream's Settings → Destinations page
First time streaming on YouTube?
Go to YouTube Studio → click "Go Live" → YouTube will ask you to verify your channel. Once verified, wait 24 hours before attempting to connect OAuth in VistterStream.

Step-by-Step Setup

1

Add a YouTube OAuth destination

Go to Settings → Destinations → click + Add Destination. Select YouTube Live OAuth as the platform. Give it a name (e.g., "My YouTube Channel") and click Create.

2

Click "Connect OAuth"

On your new destination card, click the red Connect OAuth button. A popup window will open asking you to sign in to Google.

3

Sign in and grant permissions

Sign in with the Google account that owns your YouTube channel. Google will ask you to allow VistterStream to manage your YouTube live streams. Click Allow.

4

Wait for the popup to close

After granting permission, the popup will close automatically and your destination card will update to show Connected. If it doesn't update within a few seconds, click Refresh Status.

5

You're ready to stream!

When you start a timeline that uses this destination, VistterStream will automatically create a YouTube Live broadcast and start streaming. No need to touch YouTube Studio.

Understanding the Status

Your destination card shows the current connection status:

  • Connected — OAuth is active and ready. VistterStream can create broadcasts on your behalf.
  • Not connected — You need to click "Connect OAuth" to authorize.
  • Token expires — OAuth tokens refresh automatically. If a token expires and can't be refreshed, reconnect by clicking "Reconnect OAuth".

Managing Broadcasts

Once connected, you have these options on the destination card:

  • Create New Broadcast — Manually create a YouTube Live broadcast ahead of time. You'll be prompted for a title, description, and settings.
  • Reconnect OAuth — Re-authorize if your connection has expired or you want to switch accounts.
  • Disconnect — Remove the OAuth connection. The destination remains but can no longer create broadcasts automatically.
Automatic broadcast creation:
When you start a timeline with an OAuth destination, VistterStream automatically creates a broadcast using the timeline's broadcast metadata (title, description, tags, privacy). You don't need to create one manually unless you want to set it up in advance.

Troubleshooting

The popup closed but status still says "Not connected"

Click Refresh Status on the destination card. If it still shows as not connected, try clicking Connect OAuth again. Make sure you allowed all requested permissions when Google asked.

I get an error saying "This app isn't verified"

This is normal for apps in testing mode. Click Advanced → Go to VistterStream (unsafe) to proceed. Your data is only sent between your VistterStream server and Google — no third parties are involved.

My stream starts but doesn't appear on YouTube

Check that your YouTube channel has live streaming enabled. Go to YouTube Studio → Settings → Channel → Feature eligibility → Live streaming must show "Enabled". New channels need to wait 24 hours after verification.

I want to stream to a different YouTube account

Click Disconnect on the destination card, then click Connect OAuth again. When the Google sign-in popup appears, choose a different account or click "Use another account".

The token expired and my stream stopped

OAuth tokens refresh automatically in the background. If a refresh fails (e.g., you revoked access in your Google account settings), click Reconnect OAuth to re-authorize. Running streams may need to be restarted after reconnecting.
